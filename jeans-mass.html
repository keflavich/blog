Jeans Mass

Date Tue 12 March 2013 By Adam (keflavich@gmail.com) Category misc. Tags ipynb physics

Some notes about the Jeans mass scale using astropy's units.

Simple approaches to determining mass scales:

v esc= GM R

Isothermal equation of state:

P=ρkT
cs= 2kT/m

Setting v esc=cs yields the mass scale:

M= R G 2kT m= Rcs2 G

Using ρ= M 4/3πR -3,

R= M 4/3πρ 1/3
so
M=M 1/3 cs2 Gρ 1/3

which rearranges to

M= cs 3 G 3/2ρ 1/2

where M is now MJ, the Jeans mass. In terms of temperature, this is:

MJ= (2kT) 3/2 (mG) 3/2ρ 1/2

where m is the particle mass, usually assumed to be 2.8 amu.
